0182	Heading 4	2.2.2.1 IoT Impact Detection Sensors and Edge Processing
0183	Normal	IoT impact detection sensors are composed of elements that can detect changes in acceleration, vibration, and force when there is a possibility of an impact (Choudhary, 2024). The sensors typically use accelerometers and vibration sensors when applied in vehicle safety systems that could identify patterns of motion all the time. The system can classify severe impact patterns from regular patterns through analysis of the magnitude and direction of acceleration relative to the system thresholds. The term is applied to the initial system architecture layer responsible for event identification on the level of the vehicle.
0184	Normal	Most of the IoT-based accident detection systems apply basic edge-level processing, closer to sensors for noise filtration and the detection of significant impact events, before data is transmitted for further analysis. This early-stage processing reduces false alarms and ensures that only meaningful events trigger emergency workflows (Omar et al., 2024). In the proposed EV emergency assistance system, impact detection sensors form the first layer of safety by identifying collision severity and initiating automated alert mechanisms, even when drivers are unable to manually report an incident.
0185	Heading 4	2.2.2.2 Global Positioning System (GPS) Tracking
0186	Normal	GPS tracking in a vehicle is a satellite-based technology that allows the real-time identification of the vehicle's geographic position. In the current era of connected cars, including electric cars, GPS modules are employed to continuously detect information about the position of the vehicle in terms of its latitude, longitude, speed, and motion characteristics. This information is usually handled by microcontrollers such as Arduino before being submitted to cloud servers for storage purposes (Moumen et al., 2023).
0187	Normal	As shown in Figure 4, a common GPS tracking system comprises a GPS module, a microcontroller, as well as a wireless communication module to transmit GPS information to a cloud server. The system is highly effective in real-time vehicle location tracking and enhanced emergency response and is largely used in regular vehicles as well as electric vehicles for safety systems (Moumen et al., 2023).
0188	Normal	In the design of the EV emergency assistance system, the use of GPS tracking ensures that the exact position of the vehicle is provided instantly once the accident has been detected. With IoT-based impact sensors, the real-time locations are automatically relayed to the cloud and on to the concerned parties, thus enhancing efficiency and avoiding confusion during emergencies.

0206	Normal	Cloud computing is basically the use of distant servers to store, process, and manage data in a centralized manner for a network of connected devices. 0209	Heading 4	2.2.2.4 Artificial Intelligence (AI) Based Impact Severity Classification
0210	Normal	Artificial Intelligence (AI) is being applied in intelligent transportation systems for the analysis of sensor information and support automatic decision-making in safety-critical situations (Bıyık et al., 2021). The application of the AI system in the field of vehicle safety systems coupled with the Internet of Things (IoT) can analyze the patterns related to the acceleration, force, speed, and related data to determine the intensity of a vehicular crash.
0211	Normal	Recent research has indicated that models developed using AI are able to accurately determine the severity of traffic accidents by incorporating parameters such as the dynamics of the involved vehicles, the type of accident, and geographical details. For instance, the study carried out by Mostafa et al. in 2025 proposed an AI model that accurately predicted the traffic accident severity by identifying the levels of accident severity.
0212	Normal	In the proposed scheme for providing EV emergency assistance services, the analysis related to the assessment of the type of impact events considered as minor, moderate, or severe using AI is performed at the cloud level. In this way, corresponding actions can automatically be triggered, ranging from the registration of an incident to an urgent notification for an emergency, with reduced latency independent of human analysis.
0213	Heading 4	2.2.2.5 Mobile Application Alerts / Push Notifications
0214	Normal	Mobile app alerts are real-time notifications delivered to a user's smartphone to warn them or their emergency contacts about critical events. Hence, this feature is indispensable in modern IoT-based safety systems. In EV emergency response, alerts act as the communication bridge between the vehicle, the cloud, and the people who must take immediate action (Weichbroth, 2024). When IoT sensors detect severe impact, the system can automatically trigger alerts in the case of the driver being unconscious or unable to use the phone. This prevents delays that happen with manual reporting (Omar et al., 2024). Correspondingly, most research studies on IoT public safety systems show that the use of semi-automatic cloud-connected alert systems improves response coordination and minimizes life-threatening intervals within emergency situations (Zhang et al., 2025). Additionally, push notification features allow the system to provide instant, vital information such as the actual GPS position, severity, and timestamp of the impact to family members, service centers, or emergency responders. Because many EV users already charge, monitor, and navigate using mobile applications, such emergency alert systems within the same platform will improve safety and usability (Duke, 2023).
0215	Normal	The EV Emergency Assistance Platform proposes using a mobile app as the primary interface for both monitoring and responding. 0218	Heading 4	2.2.3.1 IoT Reference Architecture Model
0219	Normal	IoT reference architecture describes how connected systems collect, transfer, analyze, and use data in a structured manner. In general, these models are divided into layers so that each layer has some kind of responsibility and the whole network remains organized, scalable, and reliable. The three-layered architecture describes the flow from sensing (perception layer) to communication (network layer) to service delivery (application layer) (Choudhary, 2024). More advanced versions include the five-layer and seven-layer models, which expand this structure with the addition of layers such as processing, transport, edge computing, and collaboration to show how modern IoT systems handle large volumes of real-time data (Dauda et al., 2024).
0220	Normal	The proposed EV emergency assistance system is in line with the layered IoT reference framework. Now, at the perception layer, impact sensors are mounted inside the EV, which measure any sudden force, vibration, or unusual motion. The information is then communicated through the network and transport layers by using an Arduino microcontroller with wireless communication to the cloud. When the information reaches the cloud layer, it is stored and further processed, where analysis using AI determines the level of impact and the action to be taken. Finally, in the application layer, notifications are sent to the users through the mobile app.

0489	Normal	Impact detection methods in vehicle safety usually adopt either rule-based or AI-based techniques. Therefore, the proposed emergency assistance system in the EV will necessarily employ the rule-based method via the edge for fast processing, while the AI-based classification process will be carried out within the cloud level to decrease false alarms and improve accuracy rates (Chowdhury et al., 2023).

0732	Normal	When IoT sensors detect any collision, sudden stop, or abnormal movement of the vehicle concerned, data from the incident in question reaches the cloud through transmission and gets analyzed by the AI module for determination of severity. 0733	Normal	To ensure full coverage of the system, the requirements are classified into functional, non-functional, and usability requirements. 0738	Normal	Charging Station Availability Support - The system provides nearby charging station information to assist users during low-battery or range-related emergencies.

0948	Heading 3	3.4.2 Hardware Design/Block Diagrams
0951	Normal	Figure 55: Hardware Block Diagram of the Proposed IoT-Based EV Emergency Assistance System
0952	Normal	Figure 55 illustrates the hardware block diagram of the proposed IoT-based EV emergency assistance system and explains how physical components are arranged and how data flows between them. An impact and motion sensor (ADXL345) will be placed close to the EV battery pack and the chassis to ensure the accuracy of the collision and irregular vehicle movement detection. This approach will prevent vibration noise caused by vehicle movement as the sensor would then be placed near the vehicle doors and windows. After the collision detection signal has been sensed by the ADXL345 sensor, it will transmit the raw data to the Arduino Nano microcontroller to perform the edge-level processing of the collected data. Concurrently, the real-time location of the vehicle will be accessed using a GPS module (NEO-6M).
0953	Normal	After acquiring data regarding both sensors and location information, the Arduino sends these details via a cellular/Wi-Fi communication module utilizing secure internet connectivity (HTTPS). The data is received in the cloud platform and processed for impact severity categorization utilizing artificial intelligence. According to the results obtained in impact severity analysis, emergency messages regarding status and assistance are derived. These messages are sent to the EV owner via the mobile application and further directed towards related emergency service suppliers such as towing operators, mechanics, and hospitals.
